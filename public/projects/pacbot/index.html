Project Overview

PacBot is a physical autonomous robot designed to compete in an annual Pac-Man robotics competition, where robots navigate a maze in real time to collect pellets and avoid obstacles under strict speed and reliability constraints. The robot is intentionally designed to be lightweight and compact, enabling fast acceleration, tight turns, and responsive control in a dense Pac-Man maze environment.

I served as the Electrical Lead, responsible for the end-to-end electrical system design, from sensor selection and PCB development to low-level control implementation and system bring-up.

Electrical & System Design

The robot's electronics were consolidated into a compact, integrated PCB that houses the microcontroller, sensor interfaces, and motor drivers, minimizing wiring complexity and reducing system latency.

Sensor Suite

  • 4 × IR sensors for wall and maze boundary detection
  • 1 × IMU for orientation and motion feedback

Integrated Control Board

  • Microcontroller, sensor inputs, and motor drivers on a single compact PCB
  • Designed for low mass, tight packaging, and fast signal paths

Actuation & Power

  • Direct motor driver integration to support rapid response and smooth control
  • Layout optimized for reliability under repeated competition runs

Control & Software

Low-level motion control was implemented using a PID-based controller, tuned for fast response and stability within the constrained geometry of a Pac-Man maze. The control strategy emphasizes:

  • Quick correction during wall-following and turns
  • Smooth motor response to reduce overshoot at high speed
  • Robust performance under sensor noise and real-world variations

This approach balanced simplicity and reliability, allowing the robot to perform consistently under competition conditions.

Competition Performance

Our PacBot achieved 3rd place in last year's competition. The robot's lightweight mechanical design combined with compact electronics and responsive control enabled fast navigation and reliable operation throughout the event.
